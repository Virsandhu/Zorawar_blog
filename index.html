places i want to visit

Singapore

1.Singapore- This island country is on the top of my list. It has secured the 31st ranking on the World Happiness Index. The country is also the 7th safest country of the world and one of the cleanest. I can vouch for that as I have been there on a holiday. It has an exceptionally low unemployment rate, and its GDP has grown around 6% each year. It also has the second highest GDP in the world. English is the most widely spoken language in the country which makes it easier to settle there. Since it’s a modern country, they have a racially diverse population and are willing to accept all.

Netherlands

2. Netherlands- Netherlands ranks among the highest in international indexes of press freedom, economic freedom, human development and quality of life. In 2020, it ranked eighth on the human development index and fifth on the 2021 World Happiness Index. It is the 17th safest country in the world and the 11th cleanest. In addition, 90% of the population can converse in English. In February 2019, unemployment was 3.4%. In 2016, the Netherlands maintained its number one position at the top of the annual Euro Health Consumer Index. Proximity to the major centers of European commerce makes it a lucrative destination.

New Zealand

3. New Zealand-This country has bagged the 8th position in the World Happiness Index. It is the 19th cleanest country of the world and the second safest. It is the 52-largest national economy in the world. English is the predominant language in New Zealand. New Zealand has an adult literacy rate of 99%. New Zealand ranks highly in quality of life, education, protection of civil liberties, government transparency, and economic freedom. The general unemployment rate was around 5.8%. The scenic Natural beauty of this country is a huge booster to the entertainment and tourism industry.

Luxembourgh

4. Luxembourg- This country has the 10th position in the World Happiness Index. It is an extremely safe country and is the second cleanest country in the world. English is spoken by 72% of the population. Unemployment is typically low. Luxembourg was the second richest country in the world. Healthcare spending on behalf of the government of Luxembourg topped $4.1 Billion, amounting to about $8,182 for each citizen in the nation. The progressive and futuristic mindset you find here is rare.

Canada

5. Canada- The country secured the 11th position in the World Happiness Index. It is the 20th cleanest country and the 6th safest country in the world. Canada has the 10th largest economy in the world, relying chiefly upon its abundant natural resources and well-developed international trade networks. In 2017 healthcare spending reached $242 billion, or 11.5 percent of Canada's GDP for that year. Around 56 percent of Canadian adults having attained at least an undergraduate college or university degree. Canada spends about 5.3 percent of its GDP on education. English and French are the official languages, with the former being more widely spoken.

places i do not want to visit

Afganisthan

1. Afghanistan- This war-torn country came last on the World Happiness Index. The country has high levels of terrorism, poverty, child malnutrition, and corruption. Currently, with the Americans pulling out the fighting has increased, with the Taliban capturing several districts daily. If they come back to power, life for non-Muslims will be made more difficult. Even if they lose, they will still be fear with the constant suicide bombing and terrorist attacks. Afghanistan remains one of the poorest countries in the world because of a lack of foreign investment, government corruption, and the Taliban insurgency. Moreover, you need to know Pashto and Dari. English is not very well known.

North Korea

2. North Korea- North Korea's energy infrastructure is obsolete and in disrepair. Power shortages occur frequently. The country has only one party. Because of its nuclear and missile tests, North Korea has been sanctioned. In 2019, North Korea was ranked 172nd in the Transparency International Corruption Index, with a score of 17 out of 100. Recently, food prices have hit sky high records. North Koreans have been referred to as "some of the world's most brutalized people" by Human Rights Watch, because of the severe restrictions placed on their political and economic freedoms.

Syria

3. Syria- The Syrian Civil War began in 2011 as a chain of peaceful protests, followed by an alleged crackdown by the Syrian Army. In July 2011, Army defectors declared the formation of the Free Syrian Army and began forming fighting units. Syria was ranked last on the Global Peace Index from 2016 to 2018. Arbitrary detention, torture, and disappearances are widespread. The Syrian economy has contracted 60% and the Syrian Pound has lost 80% of its value. Germany, the United States and the United Kingdom, have declared their refusal to accept the poll results of 2016, largely citing it "not representing the will of the Syrian people.

Chad

4. Chad- Chad remains plagued by political violence and recurrent attempted coups d’état. It is the least developed country, ranking among the lowest in the Human Development Index. Chad is one of the poorest and most corrupt countries in the world. Chad has a poor human rights record, with frequent abuses such as arbitrary imprisonment and extrajudicial killings. More than half of the population is illiterate. Chad is the seventh poorest country in the world, with 80% of the population living below the poverty line.

Myanmar

5. Myanmar- Myanmar ranks 147 out of 189 countries in human development. A large proportion of the economy is controlled by supporters of the military government. Forced labour, human trafficking, and child labour are common in Myanmar. Insurgencies have been ongoing in Myanmar since 1948 and is also the world's longest ongoing civil war, having spanned more than seven decades. The military junta recently conducted a coup d'état which sparked violence with 38 people being killed in a single day. The United Nations and several other organisations have reported consistent human rights violations in the country.
